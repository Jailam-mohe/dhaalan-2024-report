Executive Summary

The Dhaalan: National Skills and Career Expo 2024 was a landmark initiative by the Ministry of Higher Education, Labour, and Skills Development, successfully bridging the gap between education, skills training, and employment opportunities for Maldivian youth. Over 200 staff members from various government institutions collaborated to create this transformative platform that attracted over 10,000 visitors across two days.

Event Highlights & Activities

Opening Ceremony

Distinguished panel of ministers inaugurated the event, with live broadcast via PSM networks. Featured the unveiling of the Hafthaa 52 book showcasing ministry achievements.

Panel Discussions

Industry experts shared insights on emerging job market trends, lifelong learning importance, and technology's role in shaping future work.

Career Counseling

National Careers Service representatives provided personalized career guidance sessions to visitors seeking direction.

Skill Demonstrations

Hands-on insights into various trades and professions, inspiring potential career paths for attendees.

Interactive Features

QR code registrations, lucky draws, stage entertainment, and on-site job applications enhanced visitor engagement.

Future Recommendations

  • Digital Enhancement: Develop dedicated website with virtual tours and interactive features
  • Industry Focus: Strengthen private sector participation, especially in tourism and construction
  • Expanded Reach: Leverage social media more effectively for wider audience engagement
  • Sustainability: Implement comprehensive post-event evaluation and data analytics
  • Partnerships: Foster industry-led workshops and mentorship programs

Conclusion

The Dhaalan Expo 2024 successfully bridged the gap between education and employment, fostering collaboration between government agencies, educational institutions, and private sector organizations. Through comprehensive career guidance, skill demonstrations, and job opportunities, the expo empowered young Maldivians to make informed decisions about their futures.
